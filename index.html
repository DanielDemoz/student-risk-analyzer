Risk Score Calculation (80/20 Weighting):

The risk score combines grade and attendance with an 80 % / 20 % weighting:

g = Grade% / 100 (normalized 0-1)
a = Attendance% / 100 (normalized 0-1)
performance_index = 0.8 √ó g + 0.2 √ó a
Risk Score = 100 √ó (1 - performance_index)

Explanation:

  • 80% weight on course performance (grade)
  • 20% weight on attendance
  • Higher risk score = greater intervention priority (scale: 0-100)
Risk Category Classification:

Students are categorized using direct grade/attendance thresholds:

  • Extremely High Risk: Grade < 70% and Attendance < 70%
  • High Risk: Grade < 80% or Attendance < 80%
  • Medium Risk: Grade < 90% or Attendance < 90%
  • Low Risk: Grade ‚â• 90% and Attendance ‚â• 90%
Simple Rule Flag:

A student is flagged by the simple rule if:

Grade < 70% OR Attendance < 70%
