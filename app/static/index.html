Risk Score Calculation (Exponential Weighting):

The risk score uses an exponential weighting formula that penalizes very low grades or attendance more strongly:

g = Grade% / 100 (normalized 0-1)
a = Attendance% / 100 (normalized 0-1)
performance_index = 0.6 √ó (g^1.2) + 0.4 √ó (a^1.2)
Risk Score = 100 √ó (1 - performance_index)

Explanation:

  • 60% weight on grade performance (exponential)
  • 40% weight on attendance (exponential)
  • Exponential power (1.2) creates stronger penalty for very low scores
  • Higher risk score = more at risk (scale: 0-100)
Risk Category Classification:

Students are classified into four risk categories based on their risk score:

A student is flagged by the simple rule if:

Grade < 70% OR Attendance < 70%
